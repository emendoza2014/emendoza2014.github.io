Intro

Welcome! I'm Ed, your friendly neighborhood data analyst.

In the field of analysis, I firmly believe the 'why' is just as important as the 'how.' I like to know both, not just professionally, but in my everyday life.
It's more than just work. It is my passion to understand and share information effectively, so that it can be used to improve and evolve.
Growth takes conscious effort, both for businesses and individuals. Progress happens one step at a time, but we can enhance each step if we pay attention.
It helps to understand where we are, where we are headed, where we want to go, and how to get there.

I can help with that! I use data analysis to facilitate smart decision making.
Check out my work.

About

My goals, hobbies, and values are well suited for a career in video game design.
There are many paths to a gaming industry career, but data analysis plays into my strengths the most. I'm analytical and curious by nature.
I chose to pursue an education in data analysis because I love to learn. It provides the toolkit for extracting crucial information from data.
Facts are important if you want to make conscious decisions based in reality, and especially if you have a goal.

Games are so much more than just entertainment. They are the complete package. Even defining games is tricky because of the vast scope of what they can be.
Games can offer valuable opportunities for escape, or adventure, a release of mental energy, freedom of creative expression, they can provide a robust challenge to our cognitive abilities,
a chance for practicing and improving skills, a way to relate to our peers, accomplishing goals through organization, exercise, hand-eye coordination... the list goes on and on.
Games can have rich stories; they can make us feel emotions, they can be physical, relaxing, and even educational. Games are good for growth.
A great example from nature is when kittens play. They take turns attacking and surprising each other because they will need to hone those skills for the real thing.
It's so valuable, but it doesn't feel like work, that's what makes it special to me. And to the cats, well... it's just a game.

Games taught me a lot. I learned english by reading subtitles in games. Playing games like soccer and basketball contributed to my physical health.
Playing games with friends vastly enhanced my mental and emotional well being. I want to help provide the next generation with the same experience that I had.
If used correctly, data analysis has the potential to greatly influence the quality of a game. There is so much data to be looked at.
Not only factors important to the survival of the business such as sales, marketting and net revenue. They are important sure, but the 'why' is just as important.
Player psychology factors give us hints of what was done right and what can be improved. Factors like player choices, retention, growth rate,
reviews, recommendations to friends, achievements, in-game economy, fun, focus, engagement, and adequate challenge all come together to influence the overall experience.
The overall experience, of course, influences sales, which helps to fund the next game, and so on. The investment pays off.
My aim is to use my natural inclinations and my acquired data skill-set to make a positive impact by analyzing and improving games.
